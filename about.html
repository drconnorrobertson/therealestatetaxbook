About the Real Estate Investor Tax Playbook

The Real Estate Investor Tax Playbook is a comprehensive guide to real estate taxation written by the advisory team at AE Tax Advisors. Covering 13 chapters of actionable tax strategy, the book walks investors through every major tax planning opportunity available under the Internal Revenue Code -- from basic rental depreciation through advanced cost segregation, the STR tax loophole, 1031 exchanges, and entity structuring.

This is not a general tax overview. Every chapter is written with specific IRC citations, real-world case studies, and step-by-step implementation guidance that investors can take directly to their tax advisors or apply in their own planning.

About AE Tax Advisors

AE Tax Advisors is a tax advisory firm specializing in real estate tax strategy, cost segregation studies, and entity structuring for investors and business owners. The firm works with rental property owners, short-term rental operators, real estate professionals, and high-income W-2 earners who invest in real estate.

Core services include:

  • Cost Segregation Studies -- Reclassifying building components into accelerated depreciation categories for maximum first-year deductions
  • Short-Term Rental Tax Strategy -- Leveraging the STR tax loophole and material participation rules to create non-passive losses
  • Real Estate Tax Planning -- Comprehensive tax advisory covering depreciation, entity structuring, 1031 exchanges, and multi-year planning
  • Airbnb Cost Segregation -- Specialized cost segregation services for Airbnb and VRBO property owners

Our Approach

Every strategy covered in this book and in our advisory practice is grounded in the Internal Revenue Code. We do not promote aggressive or questionable tax positions. Instead, we focus on the legal, well-established provisions that the IRC explicitly provides for real estate investors -- cost segregation under IRC Sec. 168, bonus depreciation, the passive activity rules under IRC Sec. 469, Real Estate Professional Status, and like-kind exchanges under IRC Sec. 1031.

The goal is simple: help real estate investors understand what the tax code allows and implement those strategies correctly, with proper documentation and professional guidance.
