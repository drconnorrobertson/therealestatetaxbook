What's Inside

13 Chapters of Real Estate Tax Strategy

From depreciation fundamentals to advanced investor case studies -- everything you need to minimize your real estate tax burden legally and effectively.

i

Introduction

Why real estate remains the single most tax-advantaged asset class in the Internal Revenue Code -- and how this book gives you the playbook to capture every available benefit.

1

Real Estate Tax Basics

Understanding how real estate income is taxed -- active vs passive, ordinary vs capital gains, and why structure matters from day one.

2

Rental Depreciation

How depreciation works for rental properties -- 27.5 year residential vs 39 year commercial recovery, and why straight-line depreciation leaves money on the table.

3

Short-Term Rental Benefits

The unique tax advantages of short-term rentals -- how average rental periods of 7 days or less change the passive activity classification entirely.

4

Cost Segregation

How cost segregation studies reclassify building components into 5, 7, and 15 year property for accelerated depreciation -- turning 27.5 years of deductions into Year 1.

5

Bonus Depreciation

How 100% bonus depreciation under OBBBA works, which assets qualify, and how to combine it with cost segregation for maximum first-year write-offs.

6

Passive Activity Rules

Understanding passive activity loss rules -- the $25K allowance, the $150K phase-out, and why most high-income investors see their rental losses trapped.

7

Real Estate Professional Status

Qualifying for Real Estate Professional Status -- the 750-hour test, material participation, and how REPS unlocks all passive rental losses against active income.

8

The STR Tax Loophole

The STR tax loophole explained -- how short-term rentals with material participation create non-passive losses that offset W-2 and business income without needing REPS.

9

1031 Exchanges

How 1031 like-kind exchanges defer capital gains and depreciation recapture indefinitely, and the strict timelines you must follow.

10

Opportunity Zones

Qualified Opportunity Zones -- deferring and potentially excluding capital gains through investment in designated census tracts.

11

Entity Structuring

Choosing the right entity for your rental properties -- LLCs, partnerships, S-Corps, and C-Corps, and when each structure makes sense.

12

Financing and Deductions

Mortgage interest deductions, points, loan origination costs, and other financing-related tax benefits for real estate investors.

13

Investor Case Studies

Real-world case studies showing how investors combine these strategies -- a W-2 earner using STR + cost seg, a portfolio investor using REPS, and more.

•

Conclusion and Action Plan

A step-by-step implementation roadmap that ties every strategy together -- your 90-day action plan to restructure, optimize, and start saving on taxes immediately.
